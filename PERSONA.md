# PERSONA: The Low Line (subway-shame)

Decided fresh for the AAA relaunch, 2026-06-11. The V2 prior (angry commuter who just lost 20 min, posts to r/nyc) holds at its core but is sharpened: the one-time rage visitor never comes back, so the target is the repeat complainer with a chronic villain line and an audience. The group chat, not Reddit, is the real distribution channel.

## The person

Dani Reyes, 29, lives off the Church Av stop in Kensington, Brooklyn. Social strategist at a 40-person agency near Bryant Park, so her whole commute is 1 train: the F, Church Av to 42 St-Bryant Park, 45 min on paper and rarely on paper. The F has made her late 3 times this month and her boss has started doing the eyebrow thing. She is the designated MTA correspondent of a 7-person iMessage chat named "F Train Support Group" and posts 2 to 4 Instagram stories a week, at least 1 about her commute. She already screenshots the MTA status page when things melt down, even though the screenshot is ugly and proves nothing. She does not care how the subway works and will never read a methodology page. She cares that the F is ruining her morning and she wants that fact certified by a number.

## The job they hire it for

1. The 5-second verdict before leaving the house: is the F fucked right now, do I leave 10 min early, walk to the Q, or eat a $38 Lyft.
2. The receipt mid-meltdown: stranded on the platform, she opens the site, the F sits on top with a giant score and a severity stamp, she screenshots it, and now she is not complaining, she is citing a source.
3. Settling the standings: the chat argues about whose line is worse, her F vs Priya's L vs Marcus's A. The leaderboard crowns 1 villain per day and ends the argument.

Hired for vindication first, planning second, navigation never. The moment it tries to be a trip planner it is competing with Citymapper and loses.

## Where they hang out online

- The iMessage group chat. The actual distribution channel; everything good gets pasted there first.
- r/nyc and r/AskNYC, daily lurker, comments maybe 1 time a month. Knows r/nycrail exists and finds it too train-nerd to post in.
- X: follows @NYCTSubway mostly to watch it apologize, quote-tweets it 2 or 3 times a year when personally wronged, searches "F train" mid-incident to confirm it is not just her.
- Instagram: @subwaycreatures and @whatisnewyork, reposts to her story with her own caption.
- Reads Hell Gate and Gothamist transit stories and forwards the headlines to the chat.
- Her agency Slack, #nyc-commute channel.

## What they use today instead

- The MYmta app and the mta.info service status page: the official source, and what she screenshots today. It says "Delays" with no magnitude, no ranking, no villain.
- Citymapper and Google Maps, but only after the verdict is bad, for the reroute.
- The Transit app occasionally for its crowdsourced ratings, but it feels like a utility with no personality.
- Texting the chat "is the F dead for anyone else or just me."
- Searching "F train" on X.

None of these rank the lines, crown a daily villain, or hand her a shareable artifact. That gap is the entire product.

## Taste references

- Carrot Weather: a paid utility with a mouth. The bar it sets: verdict first, joke second, and the snark never blocks the data. The lines are screenshot-worthy on their own. The Low Line should feel like Carrot for the subway: a real instrument that happens to be mean.
- Hell Gate (hellgatenyc.com): NYC attitude with real reporting underneath. The bar: angry but factual, headline typography that feels like a tabloid run by smart people, zero corporate gloss. She trusts it because it sounds like someone who actually rides the train.
- Spotify Wrapped: the screenshot machine. The bar: 1 hero number, huge type, brand colors so strong the card is recognizable at thumbnail size inside a story. The Trophy card and the OG image must pass the "recognizable in a group chat at 25 percent zoom" test the way a Wrapped slide does.

## Their vocabulary

"The F is fucked." "Cooked." "A mess." "Dumpster fire." "Signal problems at Jay St, drink." "Train traffic ahead of us." "Sick passenger, every day, somehow." "The countdown clock is lying." "It said 3 min, that was 14 min ago." "I tap $3 for this." "Not my train winning the wrong leaderboard." "Receipts." "I have receipts." "The MTA owes me money." She says "the F", never "the F line" and never "the orange line". She will adopt "shame points" instantly as long as the product keeps saying it with a straight face. Words that repel her: dashboard, metrics, GTFS, headway, methodology.

## Device and context of use

iPhone 15, Safari, dark mode always, 1 thumb, other hand holding coffee or a pole. 2 contexts:

1. 7:40 AM on the couch, deciding whether to leave early. 10 seconds of attention, half awake.
2. On the platform at Church Av or Jay St-MetroTech mid-meltdown, 1 to 2 bars of LTE, adrenaline up, 15 seconds before she gives up and switches to Citymapper.

The verdict must paint on first load with no spinner theater. She arrives via a home screen bookmark she made after her 2nd good experience, or by tapping a link pasted back into her own chat.

## 5-second bounce triggers

- A spinner or blank dark screen on platform LTE. If nothing meaningful paints in about 2 seconds she is gone.
- The site saying Good Service on the F while she has been standing there 20 min. 1 contradiction with lived reality and she never trusts it again.
- A stale timestamp, anything older than about 10 min while she is mid-incident. The freshness stamp is load-bearing.
- Dashboard energy: dense grids, axes, legends, or any jargon before the verdict.
- Any signup wall, notification prompt, or cookie banner. It is a free joke utility; a gate kills it instantly.
- Scores with no anchor. If 87 points means nothing in 1 glance (no tier label, no comparison), it reads as fake, and she is allergic to fake authority.
- Cute that costs clarity: if the bit buries which line won and by how much, it is a meme page, not a source, and sources are the whole point.

## The unprompted-share moment

The F tops the board while she is personally stranded at Jay St. She screenshots the Trophy card: orange F bullet, the giant score, the DUMPSTER FIRE severity stamp, and the "Data as of 8:47 AM" line visible in frame, because the timestamp is what turns a meme into a receipt.

To the group chat, screenshot attached: "I am not late. The F is 140 shame points of fucked and I have receipts."

To her IG story, same screenshot, text overlay: "my train is winning the wrong leaderboard."

The story version is the growth loop: 3 people DM her "what site is this," which only works if the card reads at thumbnail size and the wordmark THE LOW LINE is legible in the shot.
